Controls

Grid sizeiChanges the simulation resolution. Larger grids show finer detail but run slower.140
Noise injectioniRandom flips that can seed new boundaries or dissolve patterns. This is statistical—watch a few seconds after changing.0.05
DiffusioniHow quickly local differences spread. Higher diffusion tends to smooth/merge regions; lower diffusion preserves blocky structure.0.25
ThresholdiThe local decision boundary (around 0.5). Small changes can shift the system from blobs to speckle—adjust slowly.0.52
SpeediSimulation steps per animation frame. Lower is calmer; higher evolves patterns faster (immediate effect).0.6
What to look for
  • Stable regions indicate structure emerging from local differences.
  • Noise can either dissolve patterns or help seed new boundaries.
  • The density line shows how “edginess” rises/falls as patterns form.
