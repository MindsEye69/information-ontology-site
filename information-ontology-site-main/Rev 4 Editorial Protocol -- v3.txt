0. Purpose of This Protocol

This protocol defines the authoritative workflow for maintaining, updating, and synchronizing all components of the Informational Ontology (IO) project during and after Revision 4.

Its goals are:

To preserve conceptual coherence

To prevent divergence between the text, website, and supporting materials

To maintain a single canonical master document

To ensure safe, reversible editorial practices

To prepare a stable foundation for Revision 5

This document supersedes all prior versions.

6. Simulation Development Protocol

Simulations illustrate concepts but are not themselves conceptual authorities.

Rules:

Simulations must not introduce new conceptual claims.

Each simulation must explicitly state which IO stage(s) it illustrates.

Simulations must be treated as analogies, not models of ontology.

Simulation descriptions must match Rev4_Master terminology.

No simulation may contradict the canonical document.

Simulations are updated only after Rev4_Master and website text are synchronized.

9. Completion Criteria for Revision 4

Rev4 is complete when ALL of the following are true:

Rev4_Master is finalized and stable.

Website text, navigation, and routes fully match the master.

All diagrams and sidebars are added and synchronized.

Simulations are completed, documented, and linked.

All .md files are updated (README, CONTRIBUTING, etc.).

No terminology drift exists anywhere.

Email infrastructure is active.

All historical chats are archived in the project files.

A full deployment is live and verified.

Upon completion, Rev4 becomes the official published edition.
